Personal Projects

These projects are a reflection of my endless curiosity and my passion for exploring the boundaries of experience design. Here, I've had the freedom to experiment without limits, blending UX methodologies with personal creative expressions.

Each initiative is born from observing everyday problems or unmet needs, transforming them into opportunities for innovation. These works represent my personal lab where I test, fail, learn, and continuously evolve as a UX professional.

Digital Illustration

Vector illustrations for social media and video games

Vector digital illustration was one of the first creative techniques I started to explore. It involves creating scalable images using vector graphics instead of pixels.

I began each design with a sketch in Photoshop and then refined the details, effects, and layers in Figma, which allowed me to achieve greater precision and cleanliness in the final result.

One of the main challenges was preserving personality in the character design, ensuring that the pose looked natural. The creative process included multiple stages, adjusting each element until achieving a coherent and expressive composition.

LosSúperÉ

This podcast promotes awareness about different mental health conditions and highlights the experiences of those who live with them. It was developed with the support of ASANAFE (Antioquean Association of Family and Friends of People with Schizophrenia) and the Rodrigo Arroyave Foundation. The project aims to foster empathy and understanding through real stories and accessible content—both narratively and visually.

My specific role was sound editing, as well as script editing and proofreading. Throughout the process, I handled the production and post-production of audio, including intros, outros, music, and fine-tuning all sound and visual elements of each episode. In total, I edited 15 episodes (each ranging from 15 to 21 minutes), ensuring consistent and professional quality across all aspects of the podcast.

Krunch

PetHub is an application designed to reduce animal abandonment rates in large cities by combining technology, innovation, and social awareness.

In this context, Krunch was born— a hybrid solution (physical and digital) focused on pet well-being and peace of mind for their owners. The system consists of a PLA-printed automatic dispenser, accompanied by a monitoring app developed in collaboration with two classmates.

The idea emerged from observing how many owners felt overwhelmed by not being able to stay home to meet their pets' care routines. Krunch allows for remote feeding and monitoring, offering greater confidence and independence. During testing, the system proved effective, providing a more comfortable experience for both the pet and its caretaker.
